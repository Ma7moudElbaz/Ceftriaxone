Pregnancy

  • Ceftriaxone crosses the placental barrier.
  • Ceftriaxone should only be administered during the first trimester of pregnancy if the benefit outweighs the risk.

Breast Feeding

  • Ceftriaxone is excreted into human milk in low concentrations but at therapeutic doses of ceftriaxone no effects on the breastfed infants are anticipated.
  • However, a risk of diarrhea and fungal infection of the mucous membranes cannot be excluded.
